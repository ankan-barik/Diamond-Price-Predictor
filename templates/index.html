Understanding Diamond Attributes

The 4Cs of Diamonds

  • Carat: The weight of the diamond. 1 carat equals 200 milligrams.
  • Cut: How well the diamond is cut, affecting its brilliance and sparkle.
  • Color: The absence of color, graded from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow).
  • Clarity: The absence of inclusions and blemishes, ranging from Flawless to Included.

Additional Factors

  • Dimensions (x, y, z): The physical measurements of the diamond.
  • Table: The flat facet on the top of the diamond.
  • Depth %: The height of the diamond relative to its width.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the diamond price prediction?
Our model has been trained on thousands of diamond data points and achieves a high level of accuracy. However, market conditions, unique diamond characteristics, and other factors can influence the actual price. We recommend using our prediction as a guideline rather than an absolute valuation.
Which factors have the biggest impact on diamond prices?
Carat weight typically has the most significant impact on a diamond's price, followed by cut, color, and clarity. A larger diamond will generally cost more than a smaller one of the same quality. However, exceptional quality in terms of cut, color, and clarity can significantly increase a diamond's value regardless of size.
Can I use this tool for all types of diamonds?
This tool is designed primarily for natural, round brilliant cut diamonds. While it can provide estimates for other shapes, the accuracy may vary. Additionally, the tool does not account for specialty diamonds such as colored diamonds or those with unique treatments.
What do the color grades mean?
Diamond color grades range from D (completely colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown). D, E, and F diamonds are considered colorless; G, H, I, and J are near colorless; K through M have faint color; and N through Z have increasingly visible yellow or brown tints.
How should I interpret the clarity grades?
Clarity grades indicate the presence of inclusions (internal flaws) or blemishes (surface flaws). The scale ranges from Flawless (FL - no inclusions or blemishes visible under 10x magnification) to Included (I1, I2, I3 - inclusions visible to the naked eye). Common grades include IF (Internally Flawless), VVS1/VVS2 (Very Very Slightly Included), VS1/VS2 (Very Slightly Included), and SI1/SI2 (Slightly Included).
Why do table and depth percentage matter?
Table percentage (the width of the top facet relative to the diamond's width) and depth percentage (the height relative to width) affect how light travels through the diamond. Optimal proportions allow maximum light return, creating the sparkle diamonds are known for. Poor proportions can make a diamond appear dull or dark.
How often is the prediction model updated?
We regularly update our model to reflect current market conditions and diamond pricing trends. Our goal is to provide the most accurate predictions based on the latest available data.
Can I use this tool to determine the value of my existing diamond?
Yes, you can use this tool as a starting point for estimating your diamond's value. However, for a more accurate valuation, especially for insurance or resale purposes, we recommend consulting with a certified gemologist who can physically examine the diamond.
